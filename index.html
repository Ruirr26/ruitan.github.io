Bio

I completed my PhD in Economics at the University of Liverpool. I am an economics researcher with training in applied econometrics, causal inference, efficiency analysis, and AI-assisted text analysis. My work studies tourism policy, city performance, service quality, and sustainable regional development.

Research

Tourism Economics and Policy

Rural tourism policy, regional economic growth, tourism development, and policy evaluation.

Efficiency and Sustainability

Tourism efficiency, eco-efficiency, quality of life, and environmental performance in cities.

Consumer and Service Evidence

Online review analytics, sentiment analysis, restaurant evaluation, and service quality.

Methods: applied econometrics, synthetic control, panel-data analysis, DEA/SBM models, spatial analysis, and text analysis.

Funded project experience: research support for a National Natural Science Foundation of China project, including data collection, literature review, and research documentation.
